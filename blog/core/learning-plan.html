Bootcamp Learning Plan

I'm going to aim to get my work done Monday to Saturday so I have a free weekend day to take time for myself and do things like groceries, washing etc. I like to think I'm a productive learner when it comes to tech since it's a topic I'm interested in. Foundations Learning Plan

Long term goal

My long term goal doing this course is to get a job in the tech industry, specifically in software development. Having studied tech for a little bit before this course, I made the decision to join Dev Academy due to their practical, industry relevant approach.

Strengths and limitations

My learning strengths are that I'm committed and understanding. I like to fully immerse myself in everything that I do, and help others out where I can. My learning limitations can be my lack of reaching out for help if I get stuck, I will keeping searching for answers on my own and not recognise when it's time to ask someone else.

Human skills I want to develop

I'm really looking forward to doing collaborative programming. All work I've done in past studies has been individual so I'm excited to work with others.

How will I manage my workload

I have an approach to learning where I try to complete the bulk of a workload as soon as possible, so that remaining activities can be split up over how long I have to do them and it's not a big rush to get everything finished at the end. This means I have more time to solidify my knowledge, either by helping others or diving deeper into areas I'm not as confident in. When it comes to other learners, facilitators, and industry/community representatives, I enjoy being around others but I'm respectful of their time and workspace.

Seeking help

In the past, I have sometimes left it later than I should've to ask for help. During foundations, I will use discord to contact my cohort/homegroup and facilitators, and during bootcamp I will have the option of in person help Monday - Friday.

Facilitation team expectations

The facilitation team have been amazing so far, reaching out to students before students reach out to them. I don't necessarily have any "expectations", just to be approachable in a supportive environment so it makes asking for help seem less intimidating.

Scheduling information

I don't have any other commitments that would take priority over Dev Academy. My permanent contract at work has been changed to a casual contract, and therefore I will only help them out when I have the time to do so around my studies.
